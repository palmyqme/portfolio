# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, recruiters, and tech leads in Bangkok's tech industry. They open this on a laptop between interviews, or on mobile after seeing a LinkedIn link. They are skimming for signal: can this person communicate, do they understand both business and tech, are they a real practitioner or just a CV in a template?

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Akarasiri Chaowana (Palmy), a Business Analyst at BUZZEBEES. The site's job is to make a strong first impression that earns a callback — not to document every detail of every project. Success = the viewer says "I want to talk to this person."

## Brand Personality

Technical-but-human. Precise and opinionated. A BA who actually speaks developer.

Three words: **Precise · Hybrid · Distinctive**

## Anti-references

- SaaS startup generic: dark bg + purple/pink gradient + glassmorphism everywhere = looks AI-generated. The current site risks this lane.
- LinkedIn-style template: just a CV on a webpage, no personality.
- Corporate/annual-report: blue/white, serif, formal tone — erases personality.

## Design Principles

1. **Practitioner proof** — every design choice should signal that this person understands tools and craft, not just buzzwords.
2. **Distinctiveness over safety** — being memorable is the job. A visitor should ask "how was this made?" not "which template is this?"
3. **Intentional constraint** — every decoration should earn its place. Ambient shapes, glass cards, and orbs should add to the narrative, not fill space.
4. **Hybrid voice** — the terminal/code aesthetic (`> stack --inspect:`) is the strongest POV on the site. Lean into it more, not less.
5. **Speed of impression** — recruiter has 15 seconds. The hero must transmit the full brand proposition in that window.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Thai + English audience. Reduced motion support (already wired). No specific known needs beyond standard.
